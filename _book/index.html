1.1 What you can get out of Twitter

So, you’re an R-programmer. What can you gain from becoming active on Twitter?

First of all, what is Twitter exactly? It’s a microblogging and social media website that started in 2006. Messages on this platform are called tweets, and have a maximum length of 280 characters (used to be 140 characters). On Twitter, you base your decision to follow someone on whether you think their content is interesting. Unlike Facebook, Twitter is not based on who you know in real life.

1.1.1 The friendly R community

The online R community is known as a friendly bunch! Some even say it’s the greatest benefit of using R. It’s OK to ask questions, people support each other and are genuinly happy for each other if there is good news to share. Connecting to the online #rstats Twitter community can be especially useful if you’re the only one using R in your company or institution.

Twitter is a fantastic resource for learning new tips and tricks with #R, especially when it comes to #dataviz! I love how supportive the #rstats community is. 🤓 https://t.co/9qMwbXkvEQ

— Julian Gan (@JCLGan) February 17, 2020

1.1.2 Keeping up with what’s going down

Twitter is a great source for news. It allows you to keep up with the release of new packages, job opportunities and conferences.
Lots of people in the R community have blogs, and they will tweet about it if they published a new blog post.

But it’s not all so serious, there is also room for some lighter content. Joking about the things we all struggle with as R programmers strengthens the sense of community.

Goodnight columns, goodnight rows,
Goodnight kind strangers on Stack Overflow,
Goodnight factors, goodnight strings,
Goodnight overfitted things,
Goodnight humans, goodnight bots,
Goodnight inconclusive plots.
Goodnight R 😴#rstats

— Olga Boichak (@olgarithmic) October 30, 2018

1.1.4 Making friends on Twitter (it’s a real thing)

Maybe it’s hard to picture if you haven’t used Twitter before, but it’s a place where you can actually make a real connection with other people. Sometimes you get to meet the friends you made on Twitter in real life, eg. at a conference.

Person at an event: oh hi, I know you from twitter
Me: …sorry remind me?
Person: says his name, full job title, things we talked about
Me: …er…?
Person: my profile picture is a banana?
Me: Banana!!! How are you?! So great to meet in real life!

— Robyn Vinter (@RobynVinter) February 7, 2020
